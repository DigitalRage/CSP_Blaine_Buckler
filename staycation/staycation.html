Things to do in Moab!

Fishing
When it comes to trout or catfish, Moab has something for everyone. If you’re up for the challenge, head to the sandy beaches along the Colorado River, where catfish abound and anglers can test their skills. On the other hand, if you prefer the tranquility of beautiful lakes and streams, the nearby La Sal Mountains offer fantastic trout fishing opportunities.

Hole N” The Rock
N” The Rock (not a typo, just unusual punctuation) is a 5,000 square-foot man-made cave that was excavated in the 1930s by an ambitious and multi-talented couple, Albert and Gladys Christensen, to create a fourteen-room house, restaurant, bar, and dance hall.

Moab Arches
Arches National Park, located 5 miles north of Moab, Utah, is renowned for its vast collection of over 2,000 natural sandstone arches, including iconic formations like Delicate Arch. The park offers a diverse range of geological features, including balanced rocks, fins, and eroded monoliths, all sculpted by the effects of an underground salt bed.

  • Fishing
    Try trout fishing in the calm lakes and streams of the La Sal Mountains or test your skills with catfish along the Colorado River's sandy beaches.
  • Hole N" The Rock
    Visit this 5,000-square-foot cave turned house, restaurant, bar, and dance hall—a creation from the 1930s by Albert and Gladys Christensen.
  • Arches National Park
    Explore over 2,000 sandstone arches and other stunning formations like Delicate Arch, just five miles north of Moab.
  • Beautiful Scenery
    Enjoy Moab's unique sandstone landscapes and captivating river views—perfect for adventurers and nature lovers alike.
